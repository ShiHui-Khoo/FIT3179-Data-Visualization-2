Economic Freedom 2016

The Economic Freedom index published by Fraser Institute in Economic Freedom of the World estimates the extent to which the institutions and policies of countries are supportive of economic freedom. Several areas are taken into account to measure the economic freedom index of each country, which includes: Size of Government, Legal System and Property Rights, Sound Money, Freedom to Trade Internationally and Regulation.

This visualization explores the Economic Freedom index and potential factors that might affect it for 162 countries in the year 2016.

Dataset obtained from Kaggle by Guillermina Sutter Schneider.

Here is an overview of Economic Freedom index for 162 countries in the year 2016.

Most of the countries have relatively high economic freedom index, especially countries in the North America and Europe.

The correlation between the main features are presented in a Heatmap here.

It is quite obvious to see that most of them are strongly correlated to the Economic Freedom index, having Government Size as an exception.

Countries that are of higher ranking (high Economic Freedom index) have relatively high scores of property rights, sound money, freedom to trade internationally and regulation.

However, their scores for Government Size vary, but majority having lower scores.

From the linear regression line above, we can see that the higher the score of freedom to trade, the higher the score of economic freedom index.

From here, it seems that it is unlikely correlated to economic freedom index as there is no obvious pattern. A proper linear regression line could not be drawn.

The relation of other factors, such as property rights and regulation with economic freedom index is illustrated here.

We can see that they are closely related to economic freedom index as well.

A potential outlier (a country that has a different and further score compared to the others) might be observed but that country is Venezuela.

Venezuela is ranked last with the lowest economic freedom index. So, it has a combination of lowest scores for each factor.

Since the factor freedom to trade internationally has the highest correlation with economic freedom index, let's have an overview look of how it is around the World in 2016.

Countries in the North America, Europe and Asia & Pacific have high scores of freedom to trade internationally, while countries in Africa and Arab States have relatively lower scores.
